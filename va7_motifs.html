Valsgärde 7

Motifs of Vendel Period Helmet Plates and Related Objects

The enumaration of motifs follows an upcoming MA Thesis and includes more than only the motifs of the Va. 7 helmet.

M1: Man between the Bears

Reconstruction of the motif on Va7 by Arwidsson (1977)

This motif is present on the Va. 7 helmet as two fragments of the fourth plate row positioned frontal on the left and right side.

In the North-Germanic context this motif is known from the Torslunda Patrice A helmet.
Similar motifs are found on the Staffordshire Purse and Pressbleckdisks of Eschwege-Niederhorne Grave 7.

The motif's connection to the Daniel-Motif remains unclear, but it has been suggested as the origin with re-interpration in the Germanic Context as a scene from a Heroic Lay.


Related scenes from literature (see Beck 1968, 241ff. for more details):

I. Scenes of "real" hunt ("Das Überwinden von jagdbaren Tieren wird als Indiz für Heldenhaftigkeit betrachtet" Beck 1968).

1. Niebelungenlied Lied 16: Siegfried at the bear-hunt.

2. Gesta Danorum 1.11.: Scioldus binds a bear.

3. Grettis saga Ásmundarsonar (Ch. 21): Grettir achieves great fame by overcoming a bear that eight other men were unable to defeat.

...

II. Scenes of "fictive" hunt (as a cipher for struggle with human opponents)

1. Niebelungenlied (cf. above): Siegfried is killed right after that hunting scene. After Siegfried's death, the real hunt becomes a parable in which the hero is both hunter and hunted animal."

2. Helgakviða Hundingsbana ǫnnur: parable for the battle with King Hunding
„er ek bjǫrnu tók í Bragalundi / ok ætt ara oddum saddak.“ (Helgakviða Hundingsbana ǫnnur 8)
„when I captured bears in Bragalundr / and sated the family of eagles with spear-points.” (Pettit 2023, p. 438-439)

3. Fornmanna (/Konunga) sögur: bóndi challenges Jómswikinger to a bear hunt = fight agains jarl Hákon. („væri yðr meiri veiðr at taka björninn, er nú er kominn á bjarn básinn.“)

4. Atlakviða in gr. Atlakviða in gr. : "Black fanged bears will bite with their teeth" (birnir blacfiallir / bíta preftǫnnom), i.e. the endeavour will not be easy, says Gunnar before the journey to the hostile Hun country.

M2: Binding of the Beast

Torslunda B

This motif is not present on the Va. 7 helmet, but on Torslunda patrice B, Vendel I helmet and fragments of Vendel XI helmet.

Most likely related to the motif M1 - not only because it has been found with Torslunda patrice A, but also because of stylistc similarity.

M-6 (or 5-3): "Horned" Figure with an Anthropomorphic Wolf-Like

Torslunda D

In North-Germanic context it is only present on Torslunda Patrice D.

Two Continental Germanic finds depicting a similar wolf-like figures are known: the scabbard of Gutenstein and a fragment of Obrigheim.
